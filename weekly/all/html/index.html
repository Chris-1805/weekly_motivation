Conzept

'Weekly Motivation' has one goal and one goal only: to keep you motivated or give you motivation when you most need it.
Of course it can vary whether you need motivation regarding your fitness journey, your diet, your learning behavioror just regular motivation boosts.
These are some big promises, but how do we keep up with them?

Well, firstly we provide you information about your desired spot, all in one place, so you don't have to bother finding them. Our informations are based on recent studies and of course you can find the sources. Furthermore we give you the opportunity to make calculations like BMI, how much calories you should consume and so on.

The Body Mass Index, known as BMI, is a controversive tool. In a few words, it shows the ratio between your weight and your height, the metrical formula looks like this: kg/m^2. Further down we will provide you with all the information that you need, but for now we just want to clarify, why it is a controversial tool. If you just want to calculate your BMI and check your results, feel fre to do so. The BMI, as you now know, uses two parameters to give you an indication of your bodies mass to height ratio and gives you a number. The ratio is used to determine the deviation from the "normal weight" based on the given data. Based on size, this lies somewhere between 40kg and 100kg, but you can check the table below for more information. Now the reason for the BMI having a negative connotation is, that it is highly difficult to assess a normal weight for a given size, because for example muscles are way heavier than fat, so a very muscular small person might have an BMI going to obese but true he is very fit. So while the BMI is a great index to approximate your "normal weight" based on your size, so you have some kind of an idea on what is healthy or not, please keep in mind, that the weight depends highly on the individual context.
