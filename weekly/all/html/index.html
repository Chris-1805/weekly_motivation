Body Mass Index

The Body Mass Index, known as BMI, is a controversive tool. In a few words, it shows the ratio between your weight and your height, the metrical formula looks like this: kg/m^2. Further down we will provide you with all the information that you need, but for now we just want to clarify, why it is a controversial tool. If you just want to calculate your BMI and check your results, feel free to do so. The BMI, as you now know, uses two parameters to give you an indication of your bodies mass to height ratio and gives you a number. The ratio is used to determine the deviation from the "normal weight" based on the given data. Based on size, this lies somewhere between 40kg and 100kg, but you can check the table below for more information. Now the reason for the BMI having a negative connotation is, that it is highly difficult to assess a normal weight for a given size, because for example muscles are way heavier than fat, so a very muscular small person might have an BMI going to obese but true he is very fit. So while the BMI is a great index to approximate your "normal weight" based on your size, so you have some kind of an idea on what is healthy or not, please keep in mind, that the weight depends highly on the individual context. So now that you have calculated your BMI lets move on to some facts: The body mass index is categorized into different ranges, being Underweight, Normal Weight, Overweight and Obese. In general, the same values go for women and for men, however often times the categories are slightly lower for women because naturally they have a higher body-fat percentage.

BMI Categories

BMI Range Category
< 18.5 Underweight
18.5 - 24.9 Normal Weight
25 - 29.9 Overweight
≥ 30 Obese

As it was mentioned before, the BMI is a usefull screening tool, but it has its limitations because it does not take body fat, muscle mass and overall health into account. So already at this point, we want to clarify that if you have any concerns regarding your BMI, write us or consult a doctor, because elevated/ low BMI is associated to increased risk of various health conditions. So whether you want to increase or decrease your BMI, it all comes down to nutrition, exercise and willpower/ motivation. While the BMI can be a motivational tool, it is still crucial to set healthy goals and aim for a healthy and happy lifestyle. Now that you know your BMI and what it tells you and what not, it is important to figure out your personal goals. In the next section we will provide you with a calory intake calculator which will help you reach the goals, that you set. And since we're all about goals right now, one more tip: as soon as you know were your journey should lead you, write it down but do not tell everyone else. This way you can hold yourself accountable but and you will not be distracted by what other people might tell you. So now, lets dive right into reaching some goals.

Calory Intake Calculator

As with every other aspect of life, losing or gaining weight does not just happen. Neither is there a magic formula or a shortcut. No matter what strategy you might use, in the end it always comes down to this core principle: If you want to loose weight, you need to be in a caloric deficit, meaning that you need to burn (via exercise) more calories than you consume. Vice versa, if you are trying to gain weight, you have to be in a caloric surplus, so you need to ingest more calories than you burn.
